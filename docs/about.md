# About the Author

**Charles Shaw** is a researcher and practitioner working at the intersection of causal inference, machine learning, and marketing analytics.

This monograph grew from the observation that marketing analysts face a unique combination of challenges: panel data structures with complex dependence, platform algorithms that confound treatment and outcome, privacy constraints that limit data access, and business stakeholders who need actionable causal estimatesâ€”not just predictions.

The goal of this book is to provide a rigorous yet practical guide that bridges the gap between academic econometrics and industry practice.

# About This Project

This is a **community review edition** of the manuscript. The text is substantially complete but remains under active refinement.

**What "community review" means:**

- The core methods and theory are stable.
- Some sections may have minor typos or incomplete references.
- Feedback from readers is actively incorporated.
- The PDF and source are updated periodically.

**How to contribute:**

- **Found a typo?** [Open an issue](https://github.com/shawcharles/Causal-Inference-in-Marketing/issues) or submit a pull request.
- **Spotted a methodological error?** Please open an issue with details.
- **Have a suggestion?** Start a [discussion](https://github.com/shawcharles/Causal-Inference-in-Marketing/discussions).
